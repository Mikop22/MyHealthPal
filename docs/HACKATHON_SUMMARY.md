# MyHealthPal Hackathon Summary

## Problem

Many patients—especially people from historically underserved communities—struggle to have their symptoms taken seriously. Important context is often trapped in free-text descriptions, scattered health records, or wearable-device data that clinicians do not have time to review during a short appointment. As a result, meaningful warning signs can be missed, patients can feel dismissed, and providers may not have a clear, evidence-backed view of the patient’s full condition before the visit begins.

## Solution

MyHealthPal is a patient-to-clinician advocacy platform that turns subjective health experiences into organized, reviewable clinical evidence. The system combines two connected experiences:

- **PatientMobileAPP** gives patients a guided way to prepare before an appointment by sharing symptom narratives, documents, and optional health data.
- **DoctorAPP** gives clinicians a dashboard that summarizes the patient story, highlights key biometric changes, and surfaces literature-backed insights to support better conversations and follow-up decisions.

At the platform level, MyHealthPal uses AI, structured intake flows, and wearable data to transform raw patient input into a format that is easier for care teams to understand quickly. Instead of replacing the clinician, the product is designed to improve the quality of the interaction by making the patient’s experience more visible, more measurable, and harder to overlook.

## Impact

MyHealthPal aims to create impact in three important ways:

1. **Better patient advocacy:** Patients arrive with a clearer, more structured summary of what they are experiencing and the questions they should ask during the appointment.
2. **Better clinical visibility:** Clinicians can review relevant context, symptom patterns, and health signals in one place instead of piecing them together manually.
3. **More equitable care:** By reducing the chances that pain, symptoms, or lived experience are dismissed, the platform supports a more consistent and evidence-informed care experience for underserved patients.

For a hackathon setting, the project demonstrates how mobile intake, clinician workflows, AI-assisted summarization, and health data can work together in one end-to-end system. The result is a practical prototype for improving communication, trust, and decision-making across the patient journey.

## Suggested 2-3 Minute Demo Video Structure

Keep the demo focused on **one patient story** and show how the product moves that story from raw input to clinician-ready insight.

### 0:00-0:20 — Open with the problem

Use one sentence to frame the pain point:

> "Patients often arrive with complex symptoms, documents, and wearable data, but clinicians only have a few minutes to make sense of it."

Then immediately explain the promise of the product:

> "MyHealthPal helps patients organize that context and gives clinicians a clearer, evidence-backed summary before the visit."

### 0:20-1:05 — Show the patient experience first

Record the **PatientMobileAPP** and narrate the flow in plain language:

1. The patient describes symptoms in their own words.
2. The patient uploads or references supporting health information such as documents or wearable data.
3. The app turns that information into a structured, easier-to-review summary.

What to emphasize on screen:

- Guided intake instead of a blank form
- AI-assisted organization of symptoms and context
- A patient experience that helps people advocate for themselves before an appointment

### 1:05-1:50 — Transition to the clinician view

Switch to the **DoctorAPP** and show the same patient appearing in the clinician workflow.

Highlight three things only:

1. The clinician sees the patient story in one place.
2. Key biometric or symptom changes are surfaced quickly.
3. The dashboard provides literature-backed or AI-assisted guidance for the conversation.

This is the moment to show the project as an **end-to-end system**, not two separate apps.

### 1:50-2:20 — Explain the value in one sentence each

Close the core walkthrough with a fast value summary:

- **For patients:** better advocacy and a clearer way to be heard
- **For clinicians:** less time piecing together context
- **For care quality:** more consistent, evidence-informed conversations

### 2:20-3:00 — End with the takeaway

Finish with a short closing statement such as:

> "MyHealthPal bridges the gap between what patients experience and what clinicians can act on, turning fragmented health information into a clearer care conversation."

If you have extra seconds, briefly mention the technical differentiators: AI summarization, wearable data, and the connected patient-to-clinician workflow.

## Demo Recording Tips

- **Do not try to show every feature.** One clean patient journey is stronger than a full product tour.
- **Use prepared data/screens** so the video feels smooth and stays within 3 minutes.
- **Narrate outcomes, not implementation details.** Focus on what changed for the patient and clinician.
- **Keep transitions intentional.** A simple "Now the clinician sees the prepared summary" is enough to connect the two apps.
